"Art does not reproduce the visible; rather, it makes visible."

- Paul Klee: painter, teacher, cartoonist.

My Thoughts

I read chapter five which was about expressing emotions and senses through comics. Until reading this chapter, I never really considered this issue. I have read a few comics and sensed the intended emotions without even realizing the effort that the author/illustrator had put into that. It reminded me a lot about those one sentence stories that always had a beginning, middle, and an end yet still conveyed a series of emotions by only using a few words. Every type of artist struggles to convey senses and emotions in their own way. Painters use imagery, sculptures use malleable objects, musicians use sound and language, writers use language, dancers use movement, and comics use language and imagery. Comics rely mostly on imagery to convey emotions or senses (the invisible drivers of any story). No matter how you choose to convey them, emotions and senses are necessary in any type of story.
